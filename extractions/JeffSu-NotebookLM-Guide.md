# Learn 80% of NotebookLM in Under 13 Minutes!

## Summary
JeffSu delivers a rapid-fire walkthrough of Google's NotebookLM, covering the features that deliver the most value in the shortest time. NotebookLM is a grounded AI research assistant that only references sources you upload — eliminating hallucinations by design. The video covers adding sources, asking questions against them, generating summaries and outlines, the Audio Overview (podcast) feature, and using Study Guides. Jeff demonstrates real use cases: research synthesis, meeting prep, document Q&A, and content creation workflows.

## Key Ideas
- NotebookLM grounds AI responses exclusively in your uploaded sources — no hallucinations from the general internet
- Sources can be PDFs, Google Docs, URLs, YouTube videos, audio files, and pasted text
- Each notebook holds up to 50 sources; each source up to 500k words
- The AI cites specific passages for every answer, enabling verification
- Audio Overview generates a ~10 minute podcast-style conversation between two AI hosts discussing your sources
- Study Guide auto-generates FAQs, key terms, a timeline, and a briefing document from sources
- The chat interface supports follow-up questions maintaining context across a session
- Source Guide provides a structured summary of each individual source
- Notebooks are shareable — team members can query the same source set
- YouTube video URLs are supported as sources — NotebookLM uses the transcript
- Inline citations show exactly which part of which source an answer came from
- "Suggest questions" generates relevant questions you might ask about your sources
- Notebooks are free with a Google account; NotebookLM Plus offers more capacity
- The grounding constraint is a feature not a bug — it forces you to curate quality sources
- Works best with 5-20 focused, high-quality sources rather than hundreds of random files

## Insights
- NotebookLM represents a fundamentally different AI model than general chatbots — by constraining the knowledge base, it trades breadth for trustworthiness
- The citation system turns AI from an oracle ("here's the answer") to a research assistant ("here's the answer and here's exactly where to verify it")
- Audio Overview is underrated for people who process information better aurally or want to "consume" long documents during commutes
- The YouTube transcript feature makes NotebookLM a powerful tool for extracting and synthesizing learning content (exactly like this workflow)
- For teams: a shared NotebookLM notebook on company documents, onboarding materials, or project context dramatically reduces "where is that documented?" friction

## Recommendations
- Start with a real research task: upload 5-10 papers or articles on a topic you need to understand and interrogate them
- Use Audio Overview to get a quick orientation to a new document set before diving into details
- For meeting prep: upload the agenda, relevant documents, and past meeting notes, then ask for key points and likely discussion items
- Create a notebook per project rather than one giant notebook — keeps context focused
- When sources disagree, explicitly ask "What do my sources say about X, and do they agree?" to surface contradictions
- Combine with YouTube learning: paste video URLs as sources, then ask questions across multiple videos on the same topic
