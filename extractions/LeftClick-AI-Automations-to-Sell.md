# 30-Minute AI Automations Anyone Can Set Up & Sell

## Summary
Left Click presents a practical framework for building and selling simple AI automations as a service business. The video identifies automation niches with high demand and low technical barriers, demonstrates building 3-5 specific automations (lead follow-up, social media posting, email routing, invoice processing), and explains how to package and price them for small business clients. The core argument is that most businesses have obvious automation needs they haven't addressed because they lack the technical knowledge — and someone with basic n8n/Make skills can charge $500-$2000+ to solve these problems.

## Key Ideas
- Most small businesses have manual processes worth $500-$5000 to automate
- Target niches: real estate agents, lawyers, coaches, e-commerce stores, local service businesses
- High-value automation categories: lead nurturing, appointment booking, invoice processing, social posting, CRM updates
- n8n and Make.com are the two primary platforms for building sellable automations
- A good sellable automation: solves a clear pain point, requires minimal ongoing maintenance, and clients can understand the value
- Pricing models: one-time setup fee ($500-$2000), monthly retainer ($100-$500/mo), or both
- Lead follow-up automation (CRM → email/text sequence) is the highest-demand starting point
- Social media automation (schedule posts from a spreadsheet or Notion) is easy to build and easy to sell
- AI-enhanced automations (GPT for email drafting, categorization, or response generation) command premium pricing
- Onboarding process: document the current manual workflow, identify the trigger and desired outcome, build and test, hand off with documentation
- Platform costs (n8n, Make, OpenAI API) are minimal — margin is very high
- Client retention comes from expanding automations over time, not just the initial build
- Build a portfolio of 3-5 demo automations to show prospects — live demos close deals
- Productize common automations as templates to reduce build time per client
- Referrals from happy clients are the primary growth channel in this business

## Insights
- The "automation as a service" business model works because there's an asymmetry: it's easy for someone who knows the tools and hard for someone who doesn't — that gap is the product
- The 30-minute build time is achievable for well-understood automation patterns after your first few builds — the first one takes 3 hours, the tenth takes 20 minutes
- Value-based pricing applies here: price on the client's time saved (5 hrs/week × $50/hr × 52 weeks = $13,000/yr), not on your time to build
- The bottleneck isn't technical skill — it's finding clients. The best channel is direct outreach to local businesses with obvious pain points
- AI integrations in automations (GPT for email generation, Claude for classification) are the differentiator that justifies premium pricing vs. basic Zapier setups

## Recommendations
- Pick one niche (e.g., real estate) and build 3 demo automations tailored to it before approaching clients
- Start with n8n (self-hosted, no per-operation fees) to keep costs near zero while learning
- Charge more than you think you should — most beginners undercharge by 5x
- Offer a "discovery call" where you map the prospect's current manual workflow — this itself shows expertise and surfaces automations they didn't know were possible
- Create SOP documentation alongside every automation so clients understand what it does
- Set up a simple Notion or Airtable tracker of all client automations for maintenance reference
